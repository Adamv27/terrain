What is Perlin noise?

Perlin noise is a procedural generation algorithm invented by Ken Perlin. It is a type of gradient noise which creates a lattice of pseudorandom gradients, whose dots products are interpolated to find the values in between the lattices. The result is a pseudorandom numbers that smoothly transition between one another.

Perlin noise generates numbers in the range [-1, 1] althought it is important to note that there is a heavy bias toward 0.0 so the extremes of this range are far less likely.

In the below examples, numbers closer to -1 appear darker and numbers closer to 1 appear lighter. Notice how with the Perlin noise values, adjacent pixels tend to be similar colors.

So where did the color come from?

If you look at the Perlin noise example above, what you are actually seeing is a bunch of numbers in between -1 and 1. This can be treated as a heightmap where higher numbers correspond to a higher elevation and lower numbers correspond to a lower elevation. To get a "terrain" look we can then assign a threshold for different colors. Low numbers may be drawn as blue to represent sea level while high numbers are drawn as green to represent land.
